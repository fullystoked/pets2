Welsh Corgi (Pembroke) Breed Information

Ideal for

A devoted family

Great traits

Active, happy & obedient

Potential challenges

Can be nippy

Minimum daily exercise

An active 45 minutes

The Pembi is one of the world's finest family dogs. They are the right size, aren't exercise crazy, have moderate appetites, take up little room, aren't awful shedders, barkers, or howlers.

Pembis are great fun to have around and are very playful. They really love the kiddies and have proven themselves to be the ideal pet for youngsters. They are watchful and alert as all working breeds should be, but they can develop the unpleasant habit of nipping, particularly when young and excitable or when they age and get a little less tolerant. This habit needs to be stopped the moment it looks like developing when the dog is still a puppy and more easily managed. Proactivity in preferably preventing rather than correcting the habit is the easy way to fix this issue.

All that aside, the Pembroke Corgi is a robust, healthy breed, and raised right, a most delightful companion suitable for anyone responsible.

Welsh Corgi (Pembroke) Appearance & Characteristics

Size

Medium

Coat lenght & type

Medium, double coat

Shedding

Heavy shedder

Minumum grooming needs

Weekly brush

One of the biggest advantages of choosing a pedigree dog is that you will now the characteristics of the breed. This helps you choose the breed that will best fit in with your family and lifestyle.

From ANKC the breed standard:

General appearance

Low set, strong, sturdily built, alert and active, giving impression of substance and stamina in small space.

Characteristics

Bold in outlook, workman like. Outgoing and friendly, never nervous or agressive.

Temperament

Outgoing and friendly, never nervous or agressive.

Size

Desirable height at wither 36-41cms (14-16ins, these heoghts being related to the weights
Weight: dogs: 13-17kg (28-38lbs); bitches: 11-15.4kgs (23-33lbs)

Coat

Medium length, straight with dense undercoat, never soft, wavy or wiry.

Colour

Self colours in Red, Sable, Fawn, Black and Tan, with or without white markings on legs, brisket and nexk. Some white on head and foreface.
